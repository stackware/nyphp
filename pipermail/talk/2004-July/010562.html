The question really pertains to your level of paranoia (or LOP) ... if
your LOP > 2 x the average sysadmin, then static for everything is your
best bet (even if you have to learn how to accomplish it (static arping
for example)) ... if your LOP < 1 x the average sysadmin then you can
take the lazy route :-)

I can understand the practical frustration in firewall/iptables
configuration for such a setup but I think it comes down to what you
would like to protect if (something bad were to happen){} ..

Im really not a sysadmin so Id be a bad judge vs. the topology layout of
your personal network ... but I always think straight route is the best.
~ Security wise Id hope something is monitoring your "router to the net" :-)
