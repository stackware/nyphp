Check out the U.S. Census Bureau's TIGER files:
http://www.census.gov/geo/www/tiger/. They're used primarily for GIS work,
but you can use the underlying .dbf files for streets. They contain address
ranges for every street in the U.S. So, a particular street segment record
would have something like name:Main St., left_from: 120, left_to 300, right
from: 121, right_to: 301.
In a GIS, you could pull out only those street segments within 3.5 miles of
your customer's address, then match queries against that limited set of
street segments.
