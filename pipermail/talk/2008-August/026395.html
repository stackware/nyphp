On Sat, Aug 9, 2008 at 11:33 PM, forest mars <compustretch at gmail.com> wrote:
> "The Unicode standard addresses the problem by defining a large number of
> characters that conforming applications should recognize as line
> terminators"
>
>  LF:    Line Feed, U+000A
>  CR:    Carriage Return, U+000D
>  CR+LF: CR followed by LF, U+000D followed by U+000A
>  NEL:   Next Line, U+0085
>  FF:    Form Feed, U+000C
>  LS:    Line Separator, U+2028
>  PS:    Paragraph Separator, U+2029
>
> HTH,

RFC 2821 states that linebreaks after SMTP commands and headers are
"\r\n" and makes no mention of Unicode.
