Get a hash of the data returned from your remote request and remember
it at the client. If you choose you can remember it on the server-side
as well. But you might not have control over that, say if you are
grabbing the weather from a provider.
Anyway next time you get a response back check it against the
remembered md5. If there's a match then don't update the document. I
don't know if it's efficient or not, but it would cut down on
redrawing the screen.
